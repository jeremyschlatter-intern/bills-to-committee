How It Works

Data

Bills from multiple recent Congresses are collected via the Congress.gov API, including their CRS policy areas, legislative subject tags, and committee referral records.

Model

Committee jurisdictions are defined in House Rule X and Senate rules. The model learns the statistical mapping from policy area, subject tags, and title keywords to committee referrals. House and Senate committees are tracked separately.

Prediction

For new bills, three signals are combined: policy area (strongest), legislative subjects, and title keyword analysis. When multiple committees score highly, the tool flags a possible joint or sequential referral.

Limitations

This model predicts based on historical patterns and does not account for: the Speaker's discretion in House referrals, changes in committee jurisdiction between Congresses, committee name changes, or political factors that may influence referral decisions. Subcommittee referrals are not yet predicted. For authoritative jurisdiction information, consult House Rule X or Senate standing rules.
